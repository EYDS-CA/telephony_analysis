Research Methodology

Multi-phase data analysis combining app reviews, regulatory complaints, and AI-powered insights

Executive Overview

Data Quality Enhancement: This analysis uses 10,103 current, relevant reviews from 2020-2025, achieving 99.6% data currency by filtering out outdated Android reviews while preserving all iOS reviews. This ensures business-relevant insights based on contemporary customer experience patterns.

Our comprehensive research employed a multi-phase approach to extract actionable insights from telecom customer feedback and regulatory complaints. We combined large-scale data extraction, AI-powered analysis, and statistical validation to understand the critical failure points driving customer frustration and CCTS escalations.

Research Philosophy

Focus on the 0.06% who reach breaking point: App store reviewers represent customers experiencing critical failures—the same population that generates formal complaints to regulatory bodies. Understanding their journey prevents CCTS escalations worth $2,500-6,500 per incident.

Phase 1: Data Collection

A. App Store Review Extraction

Primary Dataset

Scope & Scale

  • Initial collection: 30,000+ reviews across Android and iOS platforms
  • Providers: Rogers and Bell mobile applications
  • Timeframe: Originally 2010-2025, filtered to 2020-2025 for relevance
  • Final dataset: 10,103 high-quality, deduplicated reviews

Quality Filtering Process

  1. Temporal filtering: Removed 2,790 outdated Android reviews (pre-2020)
  2. Platform optimization: Retained all iOS reviews (already current: 2023-2025)
  3. Deduplication: Eliminated duplicate entries and spam content
  4. Validation: Verified data integrity and completeness

B. CCTS Complaint Analysis

Validation Dataset

Regulatory Complaint Data

  • Source: Commission for Complaints for Telecom-television Services (CCTS)
  • Period: August 2024 - January 2025 (6 months)
  • Volume: 15,913 formal complaints
  • Providers: All major Canadian telecom providers

Cross-Validation Purpose

CCTS complaints represent the escalation endpoint of customer frustration. By analyzing both app reviews and regulatory complaints, we identify the failure patterns that drive customers from app-level frustration to formal regulatory action.

Methodology Assessment

Methodological Strengths

  • Large scale: 10,103 individual reviews provide statistical significance
  • Current relevance: 99.6% data currency ensures contemporary insights
  • AI consistency: Standardized analysis eliminates human bias
  • Cross-validation: App review patterns validated against CCTS complaints
  • Multi-dimensional: Sentiment, issues, features, and service impact all analyzed
  • Business focus: Analysis targets actionable CX improvements

Acknowledged Limitations

  • Selection bias: Only customers who write reviews are captured (estimated 0.06% of user base)
  • Platform skew: Android reviews (89.8%) may not fully represent iOS experience
  • Temporal gaps: Analysis covers 2020-2025; pre-2020 patterns excluded
  • Provider scope: Limited to Rogers and Bell; other providers not included
  • Cost estimates: CCTS complaint costs based on industry averages, not provider-specific data
